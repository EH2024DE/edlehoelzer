Care

How to bring your cutting board back to life.

A good board can take a lot. But even solid wood needs proper care from time to time. Here we show you how to refresh the surface, sand it properly and oil it again – clean, simple and without nonsense.

The video shows the core process.

First sanding, then wiping, then oiling. No gimmicks. Just the clean way back to a strong surface.

What you need

  • 80 grit sandpaper for the first pass
  • 120 grit sandpaper for refining
  • Optional 240 grit for a finer finish
  • A clean cloth
  • Water for wiping between passes
  • A linseed oil finish or our care balm

Step by step: how to restore your board

01

Clean it and let it dry

The board must be clean and dry before sanding. Starting on a damp surface is pointless.

02

Start with 80 grit

The first sanding pass removes marks, stains and rough areas. This is where the real work starts.

03

Sand across the grain as well

After the first pass, sanding across the direction once can help level the surface more evenly.

04

Wipe between sanding passes

Remove dust, check the surface, then continue. Oiling sanding dust into the board is a bad idea.

05

Refine with 120 grit

Now the surface gets cleaner and calmer. For many boards this is enough. If you want it finer, go up to 240 grit.

06

Apply oil or care balm

Finish the board with a thin coat, let it soak in and wipe off the excess. Wood needs care, not drowning.

07

Let it dry and enjoy the result

The difference is visible immediately. The board looks richer, cleaner and more balanced again.

What to avoid

No dishwasher. Do not soak it. Do not leave it on a wet surface. Do not use random oils. And do not sand it like you are trying to remove half the board.

When we should take over

If the board is heavily worn, deeply damaged, warped or you simply do not have the time, we can restore it for you. You do not have to do everything yourself. You just need to know when it makes sense.

In short

Clean. Dry. 80 grit. 120 grit. Optional 240 grit. Wipe. Oil. Done. If you do that regularly, your board will stay strong for a long time. If not, we are here.
