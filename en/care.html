            <h2>The video shows the core process.</h2>

            <p>

              First sanding, then wiping, then oiling. No gimmicks. Just the clean way back to a strong surface.

            </p>


            <div class="pflege-block" style="margin-top:22px;">

              <h2>What you need</h2>

              <ul class="pflege-list">

                <li>80 grit sandpaper for the first pass</li>

                <li>120 grit sandpaper for refining</li>

                <li>Optional 240 grit for a finer finish</li>

                <li>A clean cloth</li>

                <li>Water for wiping between passes</li>

                <li>A linseed oil finish or our care balm</li>

              </ul>

            </div>

          </div>

        </div>


        <div class="pflege-card" style="margin-top:26px;">

          <h2>Step by step: how to restore your board</h2>

          <div class="pflege-steps">

            <div class="pflege-step">

              <div class="pflege-step__num">01</div>

              <div>

                <h3>Clean it and let it dry</h3>

                <p>The board must be clean and dry before sanding. Starting on a damp surface is pointless.</p>

              </div>

            </div>


            <div class="pflege-step">

              <div class="pflege-step__num">02</div>

              <div>

                <h3>Start with 80 grit</h3>

                <p>The first sanding pass removes marks, stains and rough areas. This is where the real work starts.</p>

              </div>

            </div>


            <div class="pflege-step">

              <div class="pflege-step__num">03</div>

              <div>

                <h3>Sand across the grain as well</h3>

                <p>After the first pass, sanding across the direction once can help level the surface more evenly.</p>

              </div>

            </div>


            <div class="pflege-step">

              <div class="pflege-step__num">04</div>

              <div>

                <h3>Wipe between sanding passes</h3>

                <p>Remove dust, check the surface, then continue. Oiling sanding dust into the board is a bad idea.</p>

              </div>

            </div>


            <div class="pflege-step">

              <div class="pflege-step__num">05</div>

              <div>

                <h3>Refine with 120 grit</h3>

                <p>Now the surface gets cleaner and calmer. For many boards this is enough. If you want it finer, go up to 240 grit.</p>

              </div>

            </div>


            <div class="pflege-step">

              <div class="pflege-step__num">06</div>

              <div>

                <h3>Apply oil or care balm</h3>

                <p>Finish the board with a thin coat, let it soak in and wipe off the excess. Wood needs care, not drowning.</p>

              </div>

            </div>


            <div class="pflege-step">

              <div class="pflege-step__num">07</div>

              <div>

                <h3>Let it dry and enjoy the result</h3>

                <p>The difference is visible immediately. The board looks richer, cleaner and more balanced again.</p>

              </div>

            </div>

          </div>

        </div>


        <div class="pflege-boxes">

          <div class="pflege-note">

            <h3>What to avoid</h3>

            <p>

              No dishwasher. Do not soak it. Do not leave it on a wet surface.

              Do not use random oils. And do not sand it like you are trying to remove half the board.

            </p>

          </div>


          <div class="pflege-note">

            <h3>When we should take over</h3>

            <p>

              If the board is heavily worn, deeply damaged, warped or you simply do not have the time, we can restore it for you.

              You do not have to do everything yourself. You just need to know when it makes sense.

            </p>

          </div>

        </div>


        <div class="pflege-card" style="margin-top:22px;">

          <h2>In short</h2>

          <p>

            Clean. Dry. 80 grit. 120 grit. Optional 240 grit. Wipe. Oil. Done.

            If you do that regularly, your board will stay strong for a long time. If not, we are here.

          </p>
